The Network

For fans and haters networks, we connected the books to the users who rated them high and low respectively. In this way, we reached two bipartite graphs which are shown below. By projecting this two graphs on books, the fans network and haters network have created. In fans and haters network, two books are connected if the same person rated them both high (in fans network) or low (in haters network). Also, Weight of the edges indicates the number of people who rated both books at the same level. Fans and haters networks are explained in more detail in their sections.

The data we used

We used a subset of Goodbooks-10k from Github, which contains most rated books, their user ratings and all the tags users give them. However we wanted even more information so we can delve into text analysis. Therefore we wrote a python script to download and extract book descriptions and reviews for selected 3000 books directly from Goodreads. In total our dataset is ~100MB. You can download Goodbooks-10k and CSV with book descriptions and reviews to try to play with the data. If you want to see more details about how we processed and analysed our data, you can find those in the Explainer notebook.
